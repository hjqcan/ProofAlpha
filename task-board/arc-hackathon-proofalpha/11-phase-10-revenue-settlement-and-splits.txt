Phase 10: Revenue Settlement and Splits
=======================================

Status
------
[DONE]


Intent
------
Show how the agent becomes a revenue-generating market entry point: subscription
fees, builder-attributed flow, and strategy/provider splits are recorded on Arc.


Accepted Scope
--------------
- Revenue settlement model.
- Event-only RevenueSettlement contract integration.
- Local revenue journal.
- Demo settlement command.
- WebApp/API read model.


Out of Scope
------------
- Production accounting.
- Tax reporting.
- Mainnet distribution.
- Actual revenue distribution unless implemented and tested.


Revenue Sources
---------------
- `SubscriptionFee`
- `BuilderAttributedFlow`
- `StrategyMarketplaceFee`
- `ManualDemoSettlement`

Recipients:

- agent owner
- strategy author
- platform
- referrer, optional
- treasury, optional

Completion Notes
----------------
- ArcSettlement now owns revenue contracts, deterministic split calculation,
  idempotent record semantics, local JSON journal storage, Hardhat
  `RevenueSettlement` publisher integration, and API/CLI entry points.
- Split math converts USDC to integer micro-USDC, rejects sub-micro values, and
  distributes any remainder deterministically so local split rows always sum to
  gross revenue.
- Manual demo settlements must be marked simulated; subscription/builder source
  records can link to source transaction hashes and signal/execution evidence.
- `Revenue.Shares` defaults are resolved in application code rather than as a
  mutable options collection, avoiding ConfigurationBinder duplicate-share
  accumulation when appsettings also configures split recipients.
- WebApp reads `/api/arc/revenue`; when a journal exists it shows the latest
  settlement status, transaction/hash, and recipient split rows, otherwise it
  retains the Phase 9 simulated/testnet summary.
